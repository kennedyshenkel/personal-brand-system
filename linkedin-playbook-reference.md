## 1. Algorithm Fundamentals

**Distribution model (four stages):**
1. Quality Check (first 60 min): initial quality assessment
2. Golden Window (first 2 hrs): engagement velocity determines reach
3. 8-Hour Review: sustained engagement extends distribution
4. Final Push (24 hrs): top performers get additional distribution. Long tail can extend 2-3 weeks.

**Signal priority order:**
1. Personal relevance (topic match + relationship strength)
2. Dwell time (how long they read/swipe)
3. Save rate (top algorithmic signal, indicates future value)
4. Substantive comments (15+ words = ~2.5x weight of short reactions)
5. Reposts (instant reposts add 5-10% extension over 2 days)
6. Engagement velocity in first 60 minutes
7. Profile visits and follows triggered by the post
8. Click-throughs within LinkedIn

**Key rules:**
- Don't edit in the first 60 minutes (caps reach 10-15%, pauses impressions 10-30 min)
- Links in body = ~50% reach drop
- Comments 15+ words carry ~2.5x more weight than short reactions
- Organic company-page reach has dropped to ~1-2% of followers; personal profiles dominate
- AI-generated content without human refinement shows ~43% lower engagement

## 2. Post Formatting Rules

- **Hook (first line)**: aim for punchy and short, under 45 characters ideal. This is a structural target, not a rigid character count.
- **"See more" window (first 3 lines)**: your full value proposition must land before the cutoff. The hook grabs attention; the next two lines validate the click.
- **Body**: 800-1,300 characters for text posts.
- **Paragraphs and white space**: generous line breaks every 1-3 sentences for mobile readability (75% of LinkedIn usage is mobile). Default to short paragraphs, but maintain sentence length variation (short, medium, long — never three consecutive same-length sentences). "One core idea" means one thesis per post, not one dimension. Connecting a personal experience to a broader industry pattern IS one idea executed well.
- **Vocabulary and complexity**: use accessible vocabulary (no jargon without explanation, no academic sentence density), but do not sacrifice argument complexity or structural sophistication to hit a readability score. Simple words, complex ideas.
- **Links**: no external links in post body. Use first comment or write-then-edit method (publish without link, edit link in after 60-120 min).
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 at the bottom. Mix 1 broad (#B2BMarketing, #SaaS) + 2-3 niche (#DemandGen, #ProductMarketing, #RevOps). Never in the hook. Posts with >5 look spammy.
- **CTA**: end with a P.S., answerable question, imperative, or compressed insight that invites response. Never a long generic CTA. The closing must add energy or agency, not restate the post. Reply to comments within 30 minutes.

## 3. Hook Formulas

Six structural patterns to rotate. The examples below show the pattern, not a character-count target. The real constraint is landing the hook before the "see more" cutoff.

1. **Curiosity gap / open loop**: "I stopped [common activity] 6 months ago. Here's what happened."
2. **Contrarian**: "Unpopular opinion: [conventional wisdom] is killing your [outcome]."
3. **Specific number + outcome**: "I rewrote one sentence. Reply rate went from 2% to 11%."
4. **Stakes / loss**: "I lost a $50K deal because of one sentence in my profile."
5. **Counterintuitive lesson**: "My worst campaign taught me more than my best one."
6. **Audience POV**: "Dear [specific role], stop doing [specific thing]."

**Structural advice (Jasmin Alic):** Take the last line and put it first. Use white space aggressively. Keep vocabulary accessible but don't flatten argument complexity.

**S.L.A.Y. framework (Lara Acosta):** Story > Lesson > Actionable advice > You (turn back to audience with question/invitation).

## 4. Format Multipliers & Guidelines

| Format | Reach Multiplier | Best For | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| Polls | 1.64x | Audience feedback, debate starters | Use sparingly. 7-day duration outperforms shorter. 3 options outperform 2. Only 1.4% of LinkedIn content is polls. |
| Carousels (PDF documents) | 1.45x | Frameworks, how-tos, educational content | 15-20s dwell time vs 8-10s for images. 7.00% avg engagement rate vs 5.20% platform average. |
| Image posts | 1.18x | Visual storytelling, data visualization | Standard supporting format. |
| Native video | 1.10x | Trust-building, behind-the-scenes | Vertical > horizontal by 80%. Always add captions. |
| Text-only | 0.88x | Hot takes, personal stories, contrarian arguments | Weakest reach but backbone of LinkedIn. Wins on dwell time when well-written. |

**Additional formats:**
- **LinkedIn Live**: up to 24x engagement vs native video when you have a real audience to invite. Schedule around a hot industry topic.
- **Articles**: don't drive feed reach, but rank in Google search. Treat as evergreen SEO assets, not feed plays.
- **Thought Leader Ads**: company puts paid spend behind your personal posts. Option for top-performing organic content. Pitch CMO on boosting top 2 posts/month.

**Format selection guide:**
- Framework/how-to/list content: carousel
- Debate/opinion/audience question: poll
- Personal story/hot take/contrarian: text post
- Behind-the-scenes/trust-building: video
- Evergreen reference/SEO play: article
- Live event/audience interaction: LinkedIn Live

## 5. Cadence & Timing

- **Frequency**: 3-4 posts/week is the realistic, high-ROI sweet spot for a marketing director with a day job.
- **Days**: Tue, Wed, Thu + optional Fri. Avoid Mondays before 10 AM and weekends.
- **Never post twice in 24 hours.** LinkedIn typically promotes only one post per account per 24-hour window.
- **Best times (US Eastern):**
  - Primary: 7-10 AM ET (commute-coffee window)
  - Secondary: 12-1 PM ET (lunch)
  - Emerging: 3-8 PM ET (late afternoon/evening mobile usage)
- **Practical schedule**: Tue 9 AM, Wed 11 AM, Thu 9 AM, optional Fri 1-4 PM ET.
- **Adjust after 4-6 weeks** based on LinkedIn Analytics data for your specific audience.

**Frequency impact (Buffer 2026, 2M+ posts):**
- 1 post/week: baseline
- 2-5/week: +1,182 impressions/post, +0.23 pts engagement rate
- 6-10/week: +5,001 impressions/post, +0.76 pts
- Per-post engagement drops 18-32% past 5 posts/week. Quality dilutes faster than reach grows.

## 6. Content Mix (Soft Guidance)

These are recommended ratios, not rigid quotas. Use as directional guidance when planning. These five pillars are the planning-level breakdown. At a high level, the mix skews heavily toward educational/tactical content, with personal storytelling and contrarian takes as accent pieces.

| Type | % of Content | Purpose | What Works |
|------|-------------|---------|------------|
| Educational / Tactical | 30-40% | Earns saves | Frameworks, how-tos, teardowns, named data |
| Personal Storytelling | 10-15% | Highest engagement | Career moments, hard lessons, regretted decisions |
| Industry Insights / Hot Takes | 10-20% | Builds authority | Clear POV on trends, evidence-based contrarian takes |
| Achievements & Wins | 15-20% | Builds credibility | Team launches, customer outcomes. Frame as "what we learned," not "what we did" |
| Conversion / Offer | 5-10% | Direct asks | Newsletter, webinar, resource. Low frequency. Active selling triggers up to 70% reach reduction. |

**Key principle:** Content mentioning your company must pass the "would I send this to a peer for free advice?" test. Be useful first, branded second. 86% of hidden buyers prefer content that challenges assumptions over content that validates existing thinking.

**Vulnerability in content:** Personal storytelling often involves vulnerability. The structure that works: struggle → realization → professional lesson. Never share something you wouldn't say in a leadership offsite. Frame around lessons learned, not trauma. Vulnerability drives the highest engagement rates but is high-variance — test cautiously and always ensure the post lands on a specific, useful takeaway.

## 7. Engagement Protocol

**First hour after publishing:**
- Reply to every comment within 30 minutes with a thoughtful 1-3 sentence response (not "Thanks!")
- Comment on 5-10 other posts in your niche immediately before and after publishing (reciprocity signal)
- Don't refresh and stare at analytics. Engage outward.

**Ongoing:**
- 10-15 substantive comments per day on niche posts that are 0-4 hours old
- Each comment: reference one specific thing from the post, add a concrete experience or contrarian angle
- Instant repost your own post at 24-48 hours to extend reach 5-10%
- Reply to old-post comments deliberately to revive distribution (2-3 week long tail)
- Tag only people who will respond within 4 hours (max 10 people, more triggers spam penalty)

**Dream 100 strategy:** Identify 50-100 senior creators in your niche. Comment thoughtfully on their posts within the first 2 hours. Their audience becomes your visibility funnel.

## 8. Common Mistakes & Blacklist

### Algorithm-Penalized (LinkedIn actively suppresses these)

- **Engagement bait**: "Comment YES if...", "Like if you agree", "Share if...", "Tag someone who..."
- **Hashtag stuffing**: >5 hashtags, or hashtags placed in the hook
- **Active selling**: posts that read as sales pitches trigger up to 70% reach reduction
- **External links in body** without a workaround (first comment or write-then-edit): ~50% reach drop
- **Posting twice in 24 hours**: LinkedIn typically promotes only one post per account per 24-hour window; your second post cannibalizes the first

### Low-Performing Patterns (not penalized, just ineffective)

- **Polished corporate announcements** (reach down 60-80%)
- **Generic motivational career advice** ("if you're reading this, you can do hard things")
- **AI-generated content without human refinement** (~43% lower engagement)
- **Crying selfies and trauma dumps** without professional lesson framing (see vulnerability guidance in Section 6)
- **Copy-pasting resume into About section**: tell a story instead (see profile optimization in Section 11)
- **Posting and ghosting**: not engaging in the first hour kills reach
- **Editing within the first 60 minutes**: caps reach 10-15%, pauses impressions 10-30 min
- **Vanity metric chasing**: track ICP profile views, qualified DMs, and newsletter subscribers, not raw impressions
- **Copying viral creators' style**: extract the structural pattern, apply your voice. "Don't copy bar for bar."
- **Building only on LinkedIn**: creators reported 30-50% reach drops after algorithm changes. Convert top fans to a newsletter (LinkedIn or external) so you own the audience.

### What IS Working

- Specific stories with stakes
- Contrarian takes anchored in evidence
- Frameworks and lists that earn saves (saves make you 130% more likely to be followed)
- Public-thinking posts with specific, answerable questions
- Behind-the-scenes workflow posts

## 9. Newsletter Rules (Future Reference)

- **Launch timing**: after 4-6 weeks of consistent feed-post cadence. Newsletters punish inconsistency.
- **Frequency**: weekly. 60% of top-performing newsletters publish weekly.
- **Length**: 800-1,500 words
- **Timing**: mid-week, 8-10 AM ET
- **Notifications**: 3 per edition (email + push + in-app) to all subscribers
- **Scale**: 29M unique subscribers across 36K+ active newsletters on LinkedIn (mid-2024 data)
- **Purpose**: convert "feed followers" into "owned audience." Best mechanism for this on LinkedIn.

## 11. Profile Optimization

Your profile is the landing page every comment, post, and DM funnels to. Visitors decide in 3-6 seconds.

- **Headline**: positioning statement, not job title. Formula: `who you help + outcome you create + credibility hook`. Example: "Director of Marketing @ [Company] | Helping B2B SaaS leaders turn product-led signals into pipeline | ex-[Company]"
- **About section**: open with a 1-sentence narrative hook (not "I'm a passionate marketer"). Tell the story of why you do what you do, who you help, what outcomes you create, and end with a soft CTA. Use line breaks. Include keywords your target audience searches.
- **Banner**: 1584x396 px. Value prop in text, speaking/writing appearances, or clean branded image. Not the default. 67% of users leave it blank.
- **Featured section**: pin top-performing post + newsletter + one external proof point (podcast, talk, published article). Refresh quarterly.
- **Photo**: recent, face takes ~60% of frame, eye contact, neutral background. Profiles with photos are 14x more likely to be viewed.
- **Verification**: free, ~2 minutes via ID.me or work email. Verified profiles signal credibility and may receive subtle reach benefits.
- **Skills**: 15-20 skills tightly aligned to your niche. The Skills Graph feeds LinkedIn's expertise scoring for topic-matched feed distribution.
- **Custom URL**: linkedin.com/in/[yourname]

**Profile-CTA matching**: when pushing a specific topic in posts, change your About-section CTA and Featured pin to match for 2 weeks. People arriving from a viral post should land on aligned offers.

## 12. Staged Ramp & Diagnostics

### 12-Week Ramp (Condensed)

**Weeks 1-2 (Foundation, no posting):**
Profile rebuild, niche-of-one definition (one ICP x one outcome x one POV), identify Dream 100 (50 creators + 50 prospects/peers), draft 8 posts.
*Threshold to advance:* profile fully rebuilt, 8 drafts queued.

**Weeks 3-6 (Build the rhythm):**
Post 3x/week (Tue 9 AM, Wed 11 AM, Thu 9 AM ET). Engage with 10 Dream-100 posts/day. Mix formats: 1 text, 1 carousel, 1 image-or-poll per week. Reply to every comment within 1 hour. Track: post, format, hook, impressions, comments, profile views, follower delta.
*Threshold to advance:* 12+ posts published, data on saves/dwell/comments from ICP profiles.

**Weeks 7-10 (Optimize and double down):**
Repurpose top 3 posts into different formats. Increase to 4x/week if sustainable. Launch LinkedIn Newsletter (weekly, 800-1,500 words, mid-week, 8-10 AM ET).
*Threshold to advance:* 200+ newsletter subscribers after 4 weeks, 1+ inbound DM/week from ICP.

**Weeks 11-12 (Scale and systematize):**
Pitch CMO on Thought Leader Ads behind top 2 posts/month. Test one LinkedIn Live or recorded video event. Build content operating system (pillars, templates, hook bank, calendar, measurement framework). Move email signups to owned destination.

### Stage-Independent Principles

These apply regardless of where you are in the ramp:

- **Newsletter prerequisites**: 4-6 weeks of consistent feed-post cadence before launching. Newsletters punish inconsistency.
- **Thought Leader Ads**: pitch CMO on boosting top 2 organic posts/month. High-ROI option for highest-performing content.
- **Sequencing**: don't scale formats until you have data on what's working. Then repurpose top performers into different formats.
- **Diagnostics**: if impressions/post are <1,000 after 8 weeks of consistent posting, hook quality is the most likely problem. If commenting volume is high but posts flop, topic relevance is off — narrow the niche further.
